Elements & Principles of Design

Elements of Design

Line

Lines are marks moving between points that can vary in width, direction, and length. They can be used to create texture, movement, and form.

Analysis: In Van Gogh's "Starry Night," swirling lines create movement and energy in the sky.

Shape

Shapes are two-dimensional areas with defined boundaries. They can be geometric or organic.

Analysis: Mondrian's compositions use geometric shapes to create balanced, abstract designs.

Principles of Design

Balance

Balance refers to the distribution of visual weight in an artwork. It can be symmetrical, asymmetrical, or radial.

Analysis: Da Vinci's "The Last Supper" uses symmetrical balance to create a sense of order and harmony.

Contrast

Contrast occurs when two related elements are different. This can be in color, size, shape, or other properties.

Analysis: In "The Scream" by Munch, the contrasting colors heighten the emotional intensity.
