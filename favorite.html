My Favorite Artwork

The Great Wave off Kanagawa

Katsushika Hokusai, c. 1830-1832

Woodblock print; ink and color on paper

Artwork Analysis

Elements of Design

  • Line: The powerful curved lines of the wave create movement and tension.
  • Shape: The organic shapes of the waves contrast with the geometric shape of Mount Fuji.
  • Color: The limited color palette creates harmony, with the blue dominating the composition.

Principles of Design

  • Balance: The large wave on the left is balanced by Mount Fuji on the right.
  • Contrast: The dark blue of the wave contrasts with the pale background.
  • Movement: The wave appears to be crashing down, creating a sense of motion.

Personal Interpretation

This artwork speaks to me because it vividly captures the raw power and unpredictability of nature, evoking both awe and vulnerability. The way Hokusai captures the power of nature while maintaining a sense of balance is particularly inspiring to my own work in "photography", where I strive to frame dynamic natural elements with harmony and intent.
