The Power of Color: Art and Web Design

Color Theory Fundamentals

Color theory is a framework that examines how colors interact and the visual effects of color combinations. It includes concepts like:

Key Terms:

  • Hue: The pure color (red, blue, yellow, etc.)
  • Value: The lightness or darkness of a color
  • Saturation: The intensity or purity of a color
  • Complementary: Colors opposite on the wheel
  • Analogous: Colors next to each other

Color in Famous Artworks

The Scream by Edvard Munch

The intense, unnatural colors (particularly the orange sky) contribute to the feeling of anxiety and emotional distress in the painting.

Color in Web Design

Just as in traditional art, color plays a crucial role in web design by:

  • Establishing brand identity
  • Guiding user attention
  • Creating emotional responses
  • Improving readability and usability

In my artwork showcase, I've organized pieces by color temperature to create visual flow and a cohesive viewing experience. Warmer-toned pieces, such as those featuring reds, oranges, and yellows, are grouped together to evoke energy and warmth, while cooler-toned works with blues, greens, and purples provide a calming counterbalance. This intentional arrangement guides the viewer's eye naturally through the gallery, allowing for a more immersive and emotionally resonant journey through the collection.
